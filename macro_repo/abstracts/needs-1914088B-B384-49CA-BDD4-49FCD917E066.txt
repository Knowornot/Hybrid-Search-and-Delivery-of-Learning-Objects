The purpose of this course is to teach the design of operating systems and other systems. Topics include concepts of operating systems and systems programming; utility programs, subsystems, multiple-program systems; processes, interprocess communication, and synchronization; memory allocation, segmentation, paging; loading and linking, libraries; resource allocation, scheduling, performance evaluation; I/O systems, storage devices, file systems; basic networking, protocols, and distributed file systems, protection, security, and privacy. The course website includes: syllabus, lectures, handouts, references, projects and webcasts/podcasts, which play using free versions of RealPlayer. Most lectures have a web/podcast, relevant reading assignments and topics, and notes in the form of powerpoint slides (also in postscript or pdf format).
