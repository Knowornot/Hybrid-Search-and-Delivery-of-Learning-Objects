"The Agricultural & Biological Engineering department administers three undergraduate programs: Agricultural and Natural Resources Engineering, Biological and Food Process Engineering, and Agricultural Systems Management. The first two programs are ABET-accredited programs (each with a unique curricula) and lead to a B.S. Degree in Agricultural and Biological Engineering (ABE); the latter leads to a B.S. degree in Agriculture. The programs for the B.S. Agricultural and Biological Engineering (ABE) degree are noted on the transcript in the Field of Study: Agricultural and Natural Resources Engineering or Biological and Food Process Engineering."
